# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Lead owns the definition and enforcement of quality standards across the project lifecycle. They ensure features meet acceptance criteria through coordinated testing cycles and risk assessment.

### Responsibilities
- Define and refine quality acceptance criteria with Project Manager, Product Manager, and Developers
- Coordinate and run testing cycles (unit, integration, end-to-end, UAT)
- Create and maintain test plans and test cases
- Escalate issues found in QA with risk assessment and impact analysis
- Track quality metrics and identify improvement opportunities
- Validate release readiness from a quality perspective

### Goals
- Ensure all releases meet defined quality standards
- Minimize production defects and regressions
- Improve test coverage and automation
- Reduce time between bug detection and resolution

### Typical Communication
- Daily coordination with Developers on bug fixes and test results
- Weekly quality reports to Project Manager and stakeholders
- UAT coordination with Product Manager and end users
- Test plan reviews and QA signoff documentation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Master facilitates agile ceremonies, identifies and removes team blockers, and promotes continuous improvement. They serve the team by ensuring smooth adherence to agreed workflows and practices.

### Responsibilities
- Run retrospectives, standups, and sprint planning meetings
- Support the team in following agreed workflows and agile practices
- Identify, track, and help resolve impediments quickly
- Coach team members on agile principles and self-organization
- Shield the team from external disruptions
- Facilitate decision-making and conflict resolution
- Track sprint metrics (velocity, burndown) and share insights

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Foster continuous improvement culture
- Ensure transparent communication across the team
- Build team self-sufficiency and collaboration

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups facilitation and blocker tracking
- Sprint planning and retrospective facilitation
- Weekly sync with Project Manager on team health and risks
- Ad-hoc coaching sessions with team members

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designer advocates for usability and user experience, ensuring features are delivered with customer needs and usability best practices in mind.

### Responsibilities
- Create wireframes, user flows, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct user research, usability testing, and feedback sessions
- Define and maintain design systems and UI guidelines
- Support Developers with UI requirements, specifications, and guidance
- Validate implemented features against design intent
- Collaborate on acceptance criteria with a focus on user experience

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Ensure design consistency across the product
- Validate designs with real user feedback
- Reduce user friction and support costs

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Manager and stakeholders
- UI specification handoffs to Developers
- Regular collaboration with Developers during implementation
- User research findings and usability test results
- Feedback on PRs related to UI implementation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analyst clarifies requirements, aligns business objectives with technical delivery, and tracks value realization. They bridge the gap between business needs and technical implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Document and clarify business requirements with Project Manager and Product Manager
- Map technical tasks to business value and success metrics
- Analyze data to support decision-making and prioritization
- Validate that delivered outcomes align with the business case
- Identify process improvement opportunities
- Create functional specifications and requirements documentation

### Goals
- Ensure clear understanding of business requirements across the team
- Maximize business value delivery
- Reduce rework from unclear or changing requirements
- Track and report on value realization

### Typical Communication
- Requirements clarification sessions with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Documentation reviews with Developers to confirm technical feasibility
- Weekly progress reports on business value delivered
- Stakeholder meetings to validate alignment with business objectives
