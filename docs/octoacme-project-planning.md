## Purpose
Turn an approved initiative into an actionable plan and backlog for delivery.

## Objectives
- Break work into shippable increments
- Identify dependencies and risks
- Align timelines, releases, and responsibilities

## Activities
1. Kickoff meeting with stakeholders and delivery team
2. Create prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria (with Product Manager and Business Analyst)
3. Estimate scope (T-shirt sizing or story points) with Developers and Scrum Master
4. Define Definition of Done (DoD) including quality gates with QA Lead
5. Identify dependencies and integration points
6. Create release plan and milestone map with input from all key roles

## Sprint / Iteration Planning
- Timebox planning to agreed sprint length (facilitated by Scrum Master)
- Pull items that meet DoD and have clear acceptance criteria
- Ensure team capacity is respected
- Include UX Designer for items requiring design work
- Confirm QA Lead availability for testing capacity

## Risk & Dependency Management
- Capture in Risk Register:
  - ID, Description, Impact, Probability, Owner, Mitigation
- Mark cross-team dependencies in the project board and escalate during weekly syncs

## Planning Checklist
- [ ] Project kickoff held with all key stakeholders and roles
- [ ] Backlog prioritized and estimated (Product Manager, Business Analyst, Developers)
- [ ] Release timeline and milestones agreed
- [ ] Definition of Done documented (including QA criteria)
- [ ] Initial test plan / QA approach drafted (QA Lead)
- [ ] Design requirements identified (UX Designer)
- [ ] Sprint ceremonies scheduled (Scrum Master)

## Role Responsibilities in Planning
- **Product Manager**: Define product vision, prioritize backlog, clarify business value
- **Business Analyst**: Document requirements, validate business case alignment
- **Project Manager**: Coordinate planning activities, manage timeline and dependencies
- **Scrum Master**: Facilitate sprint planning, ensure team capacity and workflow
- **QA Lead**: Define quality gates, create test strategy, estimate testing effort
- **UX Designer**: Provide design estimates, identify UX dependencies
- **Developers**: Estimate technical effort, identify technical risks
